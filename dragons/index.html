Site Info:

A field guide to various dragons one may spot in the wild! Here we have separated them into two categories; Conventional and Unconventional. Continue here to learn more!

Types of dragons:

Conventional:

Conventional dragons have what many would consider the "standard" body plan; four legs, two wings, one head and one tail. Individual sub-types are varied, small and large, scaled or furred or feathered, but all have the same body plan.

Click Here to see more information and sub-types.

Unconventional:

Unconventional dragons have a non-standard body layout. Some may have less legs, or more legs, some may or may not have wings, and more. This general type of dragon is categorized only by not being among the standard layout.
